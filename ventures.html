Ventures

CosmoVentures is the umbrella for my initiatives combining science, creativity, and public engagement. The projects below are grouped more clearly, while keeping the same visual language as the rest of the site.

Main Projects

SensoryVerse

An adaptive learning playground that uses AI, mini-games, and focus music to reveal how people process, focus, and learn best.

Visit SensoryVerse

Games

A growing collection of experimental mini-games exploring logic, perception, and problem-solving. These games use simple rules to reveal different ways people think, plan, and adapt.

They are part of my broader work on playful learning, cognitive exploration, and interactive design.

Explore the games

SolveHive

A collaborative platform for collective problem-solving, combining human creativity and AI to address challenges through community input and cross-disciplinary links.

Visit SolveHive

Cosmoneo

An astronomy-inspired board game where players explore cosmic evolution, galaxies, and hidden connections in the universe. It is currently in beta testing, with plans for expansion.

Formats and Concepts

CosmoTalks

A series of interactive workshops and talks blending science, performance, and communication skills, designed to make scientific ideas resonate through storytelling and presence.

Bake Your Own Universe

A science outreach activity where participants “cook” the cosmos using edible ingredients to model cosmic processes, linking gastronomy with astrophysics in a playful way.

CosmoTourism

Astro-tourism experiences that connect travelers with observatories, dark-sky reserves, and cultural heritage sites. It grew into a finalist pitch concept for blending astronomy with sustainable travel.

Entrepreneurial Journey

My entrepreneurial path has taken me through Switzerland, the United States, France, and Chile. At ETH Zurich, I trained in innovation through the Innosuisse Startup Campus “Business Concept” course. At Caltech, I joined the Entrepreneurship Club, expanding my experience in science-driven ventures.

In Chile, I co-founded Casa Rota, a sustainability-oriented circular economy business. Later, in Paris, I joined the Start U Up program at Cité Internationale Universitaire de Paris, where I refined ideas and met fellow founders.

In 2024, I was a Finalist in the International Pitch Competition at Cité Internationale Universitaire de Paris, presenting CosmoTourism as a novel way to blend astronomy with sustainable travel.
