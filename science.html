Research

Astrophysics centered on galaxies, environments, and the early universe.

My research focuses on how galaxies evolve within dense cosmic environments, especially at high redshift. I work across observations, simulations, gas kinematics, diffuse light, and science communication.

Galaxy evolution Protoclusters Lyman-alpha emission AGN feedback Circumgalactic medium

Current Lens

Cosmic Noon environments

Studying how galaxies, gas, and black-hole activity co-evolve inside forming clusters around redshift 2.

Methods

Observations plus interpretation

Combining instruments such as KCWI, HST, and MUSE with simulation-informed analysis to link data to physical processes.

Broader Aim

Scientific clarity for wider audiences

Keeping a strong bridge between frontier research, interdisciplinary collaboration, and public understanding.

Research Journey

A concise progression through the work

Bachelor's and Master's research

Early work focused on gas inflows in the Galactic Center, galaxy-cluster detection in surveys, and related dynamical and radiative-transfer questions.

Ph.D. at ETH Zurich

Developed stacking techniques for Lyman-alpha emitter samples to probe the cosmic web and constrain the cosmic ultraviolet background.

Postdoctoral work at Caltech

Studied a high-redshift protocluster with KCWI and multiwavelength datasets, linking gas kinematics, star formation, and AGN-driven processes.

Current work at Université Paris Cité

Investigating CARLA protoclusters, intracluster light, and environment-dependent mechanisms shaping cluster formation at Cosmic Noon.
