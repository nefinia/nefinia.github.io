üî¨ The Science Behind SensoryVerse

From ‚Äústyles‚Äù to measurable traits

Classic ‚Äúlearning styles‚Äù are motivating, but matching teaching to a declared style hasn‚Äôt reliably improved outcomes. SensoryVerse focuses instead on observable cognitive traits that influence learning:

  • Openness vs. focus at start (explore widely vs. commit early)
  • Associative vs. analytical processing (metaphor/synthesis vs. stepwise logic)
  • Sensory load preference (energized by rich input vs. thrive in calm)
  • Embodied vs. mental rehearsal (hands-on vs. inner modeling)
  • Emotional expression (expressive vs. inward processing)
  • Social activation (fast to connect vs. observe first)
  • Attention rhythm (bursty vs. steady)
  • Outcome flexibility (adapt on the fly vs. stick to plan)

These traits are captured first through a structured questionnaire and then cross-checked with micro-behaviors in games (reaction time, error patterns, switching cost, memory balance for images vs. words, tolerance to overstimulation).

Why this matters

Instead of asking students to adapt to a fixed environment, SensoryVerse adapts the environment to the learner. That‚Äôs especially important for neurodivergent profiles, where differences in attention, sensory processing, or social pacing can become strengths when the setting is tuned accordingly.

  • Instructional design: choose representations (visual/verbal/kinesthetic), pacing, and noise levels that fit the learner‚Äôs profile.
  • Focus tools: generate profile-based focus music and study rituals.
  • Assessment: measure progress on skills that actually constrain learning (e.g., switching under load) rather than just content recall.

How SensoryVerse gathers signal

  1. Quiz ‚Üí Constellation: an 8-axis profile (e.g., Z1/M1, A2/O2‚Ä¶) yields a primary constellation and a secondary one when responses are within ¬±10% (acknowledging ambiguity, not forcing a label).
  2. Games ‚Üí Micro-metrics:
    • EchoPattern (sequence recall): visual vs. auditory sequence accuracy, interference cost.
    • Sound Focus (overstimulation): accuracy & reaction time under calm/medium/chaotic audio.
    • Quick Reframe (rule switching): switch cost, error type under shifting rules.
    • Memory Match (images vs. words): accuracy split & latency difference by modality.
  3. Fusion ‚Üí Insights: agreement between quiz and behavior strengthens confidence; disagreement flags areas to train or settings to tune.

  • Intervention study: whether adapting content/sensory context by profile improves outcomes.
  • Open methods: preregistered analyses; de-identified task data for reproducibility.
